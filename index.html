Experiences

More than a destination

Authentic encounters that connect you to Mexico's living culture and natural wonders.

Archaeological sites

Walk among pyramids at Teotihuacán, Palenque, and Chichen Itza with expert local guides.

Regional cuisine

From Oaxacan mole to Yucatecan cochinita — taste dishes recognized as Intangible Cultural Heritage.

Natural wonders

Visit Talavera workshops in Puebla, alebrije studios in Oaxaca, and silver artisans in Taxco.

Editor's pick

The Oaxaca highlands in seven days

Start in the colonial capital with its baroque churches and rooftop mezcalerías. Day-trip to Monte Albán, then head into the valleys for weaving villages and agave fields. Finish on the coast at Puerto Escondido for surf and seafood.

  • Best season: October through April
  • Ideal for: Culture, food, and moderate adventure
  • Getting around: Domestic flights + private transfers

Before you go

Travel essentials

Entry requirements

U.S. and Canadian citizens need a valid passport. Tourist stays up to 180 days typically require no visa. Complete your FMM immigration form online before arrival.

Best time to visit

Dry season (November–April) offers the most reliable weather nationwide. Coastal areas are warm year-round; highland cities can be cool in winter evenings.

Getting around

Domestic flights connect major cities efficiently. ADO buses serve most regions comfortably. In cities, ride-hailing apps and regulated taxis are widely available.

Currency & payments

Mexican peso (MXN) is the local currency. Cards are accepted in cities and tourist areas; carry cash for markets and smaller towns. ATMs are readily available.
